The four different kinds of visualizations

By group 39

Below the four types of visualizations and some information can be found. To upload your data, navigate to the Upload page.

Heat map

With a heat map (or attention map) visualization the eye tracking data can be read easily. The visualization shows the general focus of the visual attention on the image. It shows a red-yellow-green color scheme in which the red indicates no attention of the eye tracking data on the image, and the green spots get the most attention. The color is choosen on the image based on place of the eye and duration of this moment.

Gaze plot

A gaze plot visualizes the path followed by the participant's eyes during a given task. A gaze plot is a type of scan path, and it shows the individual focus of each location. The visualization shows the image on the background, overlaid with stripes and dots. The dots are the fixations points, and the lines are the connection towards the next fixation. In this way you can see the path the eye follows on an image.

Parallel scan path

The parallel scan path visualization focuses on areas of interest (AOI). It exists out of vertical lines which shifts between the different AOIs horizontally. The timelaps of the parallel scan path begins in the top and will go overtime to the bottom. The parallel scan path does not focus on the points on the image direclty, but on the AOIs of the iamge.

Scarf plot

The scarf plot makes like the parellel scan path use of OAIs. In the scarf plot you can see the areas of interest on the image as colored boxes. underneath the image is a horizontal box for every participant. The focus of the eyes are given for each participant in a individual timelaps in which the colors of the OAIs are represented as place of focus of the eyes in chronological order.
